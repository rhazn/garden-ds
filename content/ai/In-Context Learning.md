In-context learning refers to providing a general-purpose [[Large Language Model]] with examples of problems and appropriate solutions or a small number of labeled examples directly in the prompt. This approach is often used for classification tasks by providing a small number of labeled examples in domains where good training data is rare[^1].

A number of alternative names exist, depending on the amount of examples in the prompt. During zero-shot, one-shot or few-shot prompting the models is provided with zero, one or a few examples in the prompt respectively.

Alternatives, for providing more extensive domain knowledge to an LLM, include [[Retrieval-Augmented Generation]] or [[Cache-Augmented Generation]].

[^1]: https://www.ibm.com/think/topics/in-context-learning
